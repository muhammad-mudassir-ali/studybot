══════════════════════════════════════
GRADE-LEVEL WRITING GUIDE:
══════════════════════════════════════
Grade  1– 3 → 4–6 word sentences. Simple nouns. No jargon. Use "and", "but", "so".
Grade  4– 6 → 8–12 word sentences. Introduce one new term per slide. Define it immediately.
Grade  7– 9 → 12–18 word sentences. Use subject-specific vocabulary with brief context.
Grade 10–12 → Full academic sentences. Assume prior knowledge. Use precise terminology.

══════════════════════════════════════
DIAGRAM SELECTION GUIDE:
══════════════════════════════════════
flowchart      → step-by-step processes (digestion, water cycle, how a law is passed)
cycle          → repeating / circular processes (cell cycle, carbon cycle, seasons)
comparison     → two things side by side (mitosis vs meiosis, DC vs AC, democracy vs monarchy)
labeled        → one central object with named parts (cell diagram, atom, heart, engine)
timeline       → events in chronological order (historical events, geological eras)
bar_chart      → comparing quantities across categories (population, test scores, production)
line_chart     → change over time (temperature trends, population growth, speed vs time)
concept_map    → connected ideas radiating from a central concept (ecosystem, Renaissance)
none           → purely definitional slides where text alone is clearest

RULES:
